# Yarn Digital Brand Voice Guide

## Our Voice Principles

### Core Personality
**Professional but Personal** — We're experts who care about people, not just profits.

**Direct & Clear** — No jargon, no fluff. We say what we mean.

**Confident & Capable** — We know what we're doing, and it shows.

**Approachable & Human** — We're real people solving real business problems.

## Voice Characteristics

### ✅ We Are:
- **Confident without arrogance** — "We've helped 50+ businesses grow" not "We're the best agency ever"
- **Direct without being harsh** — "That approach won't work because..." not "That's terrible"
- **Professional without being stuffy** — "Let's chat about your project" not "We would be delighted to discuss your requirements"
- **Helpful without being pushy** — "Here's what we'd recommend" not "You need to buy this now"

### ❌ We Are Not:
- Corporate-speak or buzzword-heavy
- Overly casual or unprofessional  
- Pushy or sales-heavy
- Academic or theoretical

## Tone Variations by Context

### Client Communication
- **Warm and professional** — building trust and confidence
- **Clear about next steps** — no ambiguity
- **Solution-focused** — "Here's how we fix that"

### Marketing Content
- **Confident and compelling** — showcasing expertise
- **Results-driven** — specific outcomes, not vague promises
- **Local and relevant** — speaking to UK/Ireland businesses

### Social Media
- **Authentic and engaging** — showing the humans behind the brand
- **Value-first** — helpful tips before sales pitches
- **Conversational** — like talking to a knowledgeable friend

## Language Guidelines

### Words We Use
- **Growth** (not expansion)
- **Results** (not outcomes)
- **Businesses** (not enterprises or organizations)
- **Websites** (not digital platforms)
- **Clients** (not customers)
- **Team** (not resources)

### Words We Avoid
- Synergy, leverage, utilize, ideate
- Disruptive, innovative, cutting-edge
- Solutions (overused — be specific)
- Journey (customer journey is fine, brand journey is not)

## Message Framework

### Problem → Solution → Proof
1. **Identify the real business problem** — not just the symptom
2. **Explain our specific solution** — how we actually help
3. **Show the results** — real numbers, real clients

### Example:
❌ "We help businesses maximize their digital potential through innovative solutions"
✅ "We build websites that actually convert visitors into customers — like the 40% increase we delivered for Stonebridge Farm"

## Brand Promises

### What We Deliver
- **Design that works** — beautiful AND functional
- **Clear communication** — no surprises, regular updates  
- **Real results** — measurable business growth
- **Local expertise** — we understand UK/Ireland markets

### How We Say It
- "We design, build, and grow your business online"
- "No jargon, no nonsense — just results"
- "Your growth is our expertise"

## Content Types

### Case Studies
- Start with the business challenge
- Explain our approach (briefly)
- Show specific results with numbers
- End with client quote if possible

### Blog Posts
- Educational first, promotional second
- Actionable advice businesses can use
- Local examples and case studies
- Clear, scannable formatting

### Social Posts
- Behind-the-scenes team content
- Quick tips and insights
- Client wins (with permission)
- Industry observations

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, ask:
- [ ] Would a busy business owner find this useful?
- [ ] Is it clear what we do and how we help?
- [ ] Does it sound like a real person wrote it?
- [ ] Are we being specific rather than generic?
- [ ] Would we want to receive this message?
